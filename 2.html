EVERY BILKENTLI IS INTERESTING

“People are reserved around Bilkent, aren't they? This indifferent attitude in Bilkent's community became more apparent to me during my summer in Ohio, US. I worked at a winery, and a significant part of my day revolved around meeting people from various backgrounds. A common characteristic amongst most of these people was their ability to open up to a stranger. They share and encourage you to share. I felt that approaching a stranger and opening up a little was just a regular part of their day.

During my stay in the US, I gradually realized how talking to and smiling at strangers might seem trivial yet make a huge difference in establishing a community without social awkwardness. That's the most important lesson that I brought back with me.

I was not the type of person who used to randomly talk with others. I guess I smile more often now or say 'hi' to a familiar stranger because that's the least I can to do to make a difference. Just today, I saw a girl standing in front of a mirror and realized that we've been living in the same dorm for about a year now and have never talked. I confronted her with some small talk. Things felt strange for a couple of seconds at first; however, at least we are now not completely oblivious to each other. I think it makes me feel better and might even help others build up their confidence.

I would love to live on a campus where smiling at strangers is not a burden. A place where people are more engaged with one another and a community where people can open up to strangers. Wouldn't it help reduce the stress that people experience at this university?

My advice to the people around here is, always be open to sharing something. It doesn't have to be personal!”
